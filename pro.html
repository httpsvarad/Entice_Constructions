MMRDA/9/MMRP/CA48

Mumbai's Metro Line 9, an extension of the Red Line, features a unique double-decker bridge connecting Dahisar to Mira Road and Andheri to Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (CSMIA). This innovative metro line combines a flyover on its first deck and metro tracks on the second deck. The flyover originates near the projected Shivaji Chowk (Mira Road) metro station, traversing Mira Bhayander Road to reach Bhayander (West). Notably, it serves as a crucial connector, linking the Western Express Highway, the Western Line, and Mumbai Metro's Lines 2 and 7, setting it apart from other metro lines in the city.

Mumbai Metro Line 7

Mumbai Metro Line 7, which runs from Dahisar East to Andheri East, is a 16.4 km long elevated network that comprises 14 stations from Dahisar to Andheri. Earlier, this metro line was proposed by the government and was going to be recognized as the red metro line.However, later, the authorities decided to extend the route. The line 7 corridor at Dahisar was extended up to Mira Bhayandar towards the north as metro 9. Towards the southern end, it was extended from Andheri to the CSMIA T2 International Airport as metro 7A.
